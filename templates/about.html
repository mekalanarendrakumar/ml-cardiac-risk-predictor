About This Project

The Cardiac Risk Predictor is a machine learning-based web application designed to assess the risk of heart disease based on various patient health indicators. This tool uses advanced algorithms to analyze multiple clinical parameters and provide risk assessments that can aid in early detection and prevention of cardiovascular diseases.

How It Works

1. Data Input

Patients provide clinical information including age, sex, chest pain type, blood pressure, cholesterol levels, and other cardiac indicators.

2. Machine Learning Analysis

Our Random Forest algorithm processes the input data using patterns learned from training on cardiac health datasets.

3. Risk Assessment

The model outputs a probability score and risk level classification to help understand potential cardiac health concerns.

Model Features

Age

Patient's age in years

Sex

Biological sex (Male/Female)

Chest Pain Type

Classification of chest pain symptoms

Blood Pressure

Resting blood pressure measurement

Cholesterol

Serum cholesterol levels

Heart Rate

Maximum heart rate achieved

ECG Results

Resting electrocardiographic results

Exercise Angina

Exercise induced angina presence

ST Depression

ST depression induced by exercise

Accuracy & Performance

Our Random Forest model has been trained and validated using synthetic cardiac health data that mimics real-world patterns. The model achieves good performance metrics and provides reliable risk assessments. However, this is a demonstration project and should not be used for actual medical diagnosis.

Important Medical Disclaimer

This tool is for educational and demonstration purposes only. It is not intended to replace professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. The predictions made by this system should never be used as the sole basis for medical decisions.

Always consult with qualified healthcare professionals for proper medical evaluation and treatment. If you have concerns about your heart health, please contact your doctor or seek emergency medical attention if experiencing symptoms of a heart attack.
